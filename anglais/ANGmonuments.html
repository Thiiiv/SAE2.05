Medina Azahara

Medina Azahara (Madinat al-Zahra) is the city that ordered the construction of the Caliph Abderramán III in order to show the world the splendor of his caliphate. It is strategically located in the foothills of the Sierra Morena. Its name means "city of light", history wanted the city to not reach the century of life. It was then devastated by the Amohads, years after its abandonment.

The Bridge Gate

The Bridge Gate or Triumphal Arch, is one of the most visited monuments in Cordoba because it is located in the historic center of the city, between the Mosque and the Roman Bridge, which together with the Calahorra Tower, is part of the Bridge Environment.

The Roman Temple

The Roman Temple is a monument with which we can admire the grandeur of the city in Roman times. It is composed of 11 columns, measuring 32 meters long and 16 meters wide.

Synagogue of Córdoba

The synagogue of Córdoba is located in the Jewish quarter of the city, it is a perfectly preserved medieval building, the only one in Andalusia and the third in Spain. It is declared a property of cultural interest and a national monument.
